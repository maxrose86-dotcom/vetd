Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026  ·  Effective from: April 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to Vetd. We take your privacy seriously and are committed to protecting your personal data in compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and Swedish law.

This Privacy Policy explains how [Company Name] ("Vetd", "we", "us", or "our"), registered in Sweden, collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal data when you use our platform at vetd.io.

If you have questions about this policy or how we handle your data, please contact us at privacy@vetd.io.

Summary: We collect only what we need to run the platform. We store your data securely in the EU. You have full rights to access, correct, and delete your data at any time. We do not sell your data. We do not use third-party tracking cookies.

3. Why we collect it (legal basis)

We process your data on the following legal bases (GDPR Article 6):

  • Contract performance — Account, profile, application, and payment data is necessary to provide the Vetd service you signed up for.
  • Legitimate interest — Platform analytics, fraud prevention, and quality assurance are in the legitimate interests of Vetd and its users.
  • Consent — Your language preference is stored in localStorage only if you accept our cookie notice. You may withdraw consent at any time.
  • Legal obligation — We may retain certain financial records as required by Swedish tax law and accounting regulations.

4. How it is stored

All personal data is stored using Supabase, a secure cloud database platform. Our Supabase project is hosted in the EU (Frankfurt, Germany — AWS eu-central-1 region).

Supabase is certified under ISO 27001 and implements encryption at rest and in transit. Access to the database is controlled by row-level security (RLS) policies, ensuring that you can only access your own data.

We do not transfer personal data outside the European Economic Area (EEA) unless protected by appropriate safeguards under GDPR Chapter V.

5. Your rights (GDPR Articles 15–20)

Under GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

Right of access (Art. 15) Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you.
Right to rectification (Art. 16) Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
Right to erasure (Art. 17) Request deletion of your account and all associated data ("right to be forgotten"). Available in your profile settings.
Right to restrict processing (Art. 18) Request that we limit how we process your data in certain circumstances.
Right to data portability (Art. 20) Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format (JSON/CSV).
Right to object (Art. 21) Object to processing based on legitimate interests, including profiling for matching purposes.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@vetd.io. We will respond within 30 days. Contributors can also delete their account directly from the profile tab in the contributor portal — this triggers immediate deletion of all associated data.

6. Cookie usage

We use no third-party cookies and no tracking cookies of any kind. We use only browser localStorage for the following purposes:

  • Language preference (vetd_lang) — Stores your chosen language (EN or SV) so you don't have to re-select it on each visit.
  • Cookie consent status (vetd_cookie_consent) — Records whether you have accepted or declined our localStorage usage.

If you decline our cookie notice, your language preference will not be stored. These items are not cookies in the legal sense (they are not transmitted to servers with requests), but we follow cookie consent best practice regardless.

We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any other third-party analytics or advertising tracking.

8. Who we share data with

We share data only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Supabase — Our database and authentication provider (EU-hosted, see Section 4).
  • Clients (companies posting studies) — When you apply to a study, the client can see your profile information relevant to their study (initials/first name, rating, tier, location city). Full names are not exposed to clients without explicit consent.
  • Legal requirements — We may disclose data if required by Swedish law, court order, or to protect the rights and safety of Vetd users.

We do not sell, rent, or otherwise commercially share your personal data with third parties for marketing purposes.
